# Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR): Basics & Sendai Framework

---

## 1. What is Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR)?
- **Definition**: DRR is about **preventing new** and **reducing existing** disaster risks.
- **Goal**: Save lives, protect livelihoods, and reduce economic losses.
- **Why it matters**: Disasters disrupt communities, economies, and development.

---

## 2. What is a Disaster?
- **Natural hazards** (earthquakes, floods, storms) + **vulnerability** = Disaster.
- **Example**: A flood in a city with poor drainage causes more damage than in a prepared city.
- **Key point**: Disasters are **not natural**—they result from how we live and build.

---

## 3. The Sendai Framework (2015-2030)
- **What it is**: A global agreement to reduce disaster risk, adopted in Sendai, Japan.
- **4 Priorities**:
  - Understanding disaster risk.
  - Strengthening disaster risk governance.
  - Investing in resilience (buildings, infrastructure, ecosystems).
  - Enhancing preparedness for effective response and recovery.
- **7 Global Targets**: Reduce mortality, affected people, economic losses, and damage to infrastructure.

---

## 4. Basic Steps for DRR
- **1. Know the risks**: Map hazards and vulnerabilities in your community.
- **2. Plan ahead**: Create and practice emergency plans.
- **3. Build strong**: Use resilient construction and protect ecosystems.
- **4. Educate everyone**: Schools, workplaces, and families should know what to do.
- **5. Work together**: Governments, businesses, and communities must collaborate.

---

## 5. How Can You Help?
- **At home**: Secure furniture, know evacuation routes, prepare an emergency kit.
- **In community**: Join local DRR groups, share knowledge, support vulnerable neighbors.
- **Globally**: Advocate for policies that reduce risk and protect the environment.

---

## 6. Success Stories
- **Japan**: Earthquake-resistant buildings and early warning systems save lives.
- **Bangladesh**: Cyclone shelters and community training reduce deaths.
- **Costa Rica**: Forest conservation reduces landslide risks.
